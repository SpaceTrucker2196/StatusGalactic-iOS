## Design decisions

1. **Standalone watch app, not "Watch App for iOS App".** Modern watchOS supports independent installation. Avoids embedding complexity in XcodeGen; users install the watch app directly from the Watch app on iPhone or the App Store on the watch.
2. **No callsigns, no notifications on the watch.** v1 keeps the watch surface minimal: read the brief, glance at complications. Both already exist on the phone; duplicating them on the watch surface would multiply UI without adding value.
3. **Same `BriefBuilder` and astronomy.** Zero divergence between phone and watch ŌĆö they call the same Swift source. If a planet position is wrong on the watch, it's also wrong on the phone, and the fix is one place.
4. **20-minute reload policy.** Tighter than the phone widget's 30 min. Watch faces are looked at briefly and often; a stale Kp on the watch face feels wrong faster than a stale Kp in a corner of the home screen.
5. **Single `BriefComplication`.** Four accessory families share one provider. iOS widget split families into `BriefWidgetView` selecting by family; same pattern here. If we later want a sun-specific complication separate from the weather one, it's a second `Widget` in the bundle.

## Drift / parity risks for Android (and Wear OS, if ever)

1. **Watch surface scope.** iOS watch app is intentionally narrower than the phone app (no callsigns, no notifications). If a Wear OS port lands, mirror that scope decision; don't try to put the whole iOS UI on a tiny screen.
2. **Complication families.** WidgetKit's four accessory families map roughly to Wear OS's complication types (`SHORT_TEXT`, `LONG_TEXT`, `RANGED_VALUE`, etc.). The mapping isn't 1:1 and should be tabled in `MIGRATION_PLAN.md` when Wear OS work begins.
3. **Reload cadence.** Watch face complications throttle differently across platforms; 20-minute hint is a starting point, document if Wear OS needs different.
4. **Hardcoded fallback lat/lng in complications.** Same `WatchComplicationConfig` issue as the iOS widget ŌĆö needs an App Group to read user settings. Identical migration recipe applies.
